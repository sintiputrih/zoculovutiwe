How Much are Tickets for the 2024 French Open?

The French Open, also known as Roland-Garros, is one of the most prestigious tennis tournaments in the world, attracting top tennis players and fervent fans from around the globe.

The event, scheduled to take place from May 20 to June 9, 2024, offers an array of ticket options, pricing tiers, and unique experiences for attendees.

In this Fan Arch guide, we will delve into the intricacies of ticket prices for the French Open in 2024, exploring various aspects such as ticket categories, pricing tiers, availability, and relevant factors affecting ticket prices.

Official Ticketing Partners and Presale Opportunities

Before delving into the ticket categories and pricing, it's important to note that official tickets for the French Open are available through authorized partners.

Among these are Mastercard, offering its cardholders exclusive presale ticketing windows for the opening rounds of the tournament.

Also, Tennis Ticket Service is another authorized vendor offering official tickets for Roland-Garros. It's crucial for prospective attendees to purchase tickets only from these authorized vendors to ensure the validity and authenticity of their tickets.

Ticket Categories and Pricing Tiers

Opening Week

The Opening Week of Roland-Garros offers an exciting opportunity to witness the qualifying rounds, providing a glimpse of intense tennis action.

The knockout games during the Opening Week are set to be played on Court Suzanne-Lenglen, with a capacity of 15,000 spectators per day.

The pricing for Opening Week tickets is set at just €25, or €12 for individuals under 25 years of age.

Main Tournament

For those interested in experiencing the heart of Roland-Garros, tickets for the main draw are available.

Attendees can cheer on both French and international players from the stands of the Philippe-Chatrier, Suzanne-Lenglen, Simonne-Mathieu, and Outside courts.

Notably, a "Chatrier Upgrade" offer is applicable to all tickets, allowing access to the lower stands of Court Philippe-Chatrier in open seating during day sessions.

Premium Experiences

A premium experience is also on offer, providing exclusive access to the best seats on the main courts, private areas, and gourmet dining options.

Five premium services are available: "Premium Opening Week", "Découverte", "Emotion Chatrier", "Elégance", and "Passion".

Gala Evenings and Exclusive Presale Opportunities

Those who prefer night sessions can opt for tickets to the eleven night sessions scheduled from Sunday, May 26 to Wednesday, June 5, offering an electric atmosphere and great matches from €45.

Mastercard cardholders have access to an exclusive presale ticketing window for the opening rounds, with packages available for both Men's and Ladies' singles matches on the Philippe-Chatrier and Suzanne-Lenglen courts.

Roland-Garros & Rolex Paris Masters 2024 Pass

For avid tennis enthusiasts, the Roland-Garros & Rolex Paris Masters 2024 pass offers the opportunity to attend matches on clay for one day and on hard courts for day and evening sessions on Monday, October 28, and Tuesday, October 29.

Yannick Noah Day

Renamed in 2023 to mark the 40th anniversary of Yannick Noah's victory at Roland-Garros, this special event features festive entertainment, exhibition matches, and big-name practice sessions.

The "Solidarity Saturday" on May 25 offers all-day access for just €20, with profits being donated to the Fête le Mur association and clubs that won Solidarity and Environmental Action Trophies.

Factors Affecting Ticket Prices and Availability

Demand and Timing

The demand for tickets to the French Open is significant, especially for the main draw and premium experiences.

As a result, ticket prices may fluctuate based on the timing of purchase and the popularity of the matches or sessions.

It's advisable for attendees to secure their tickets early to ensure availability and potentially benefit from early bird pricing.

Exclusive Presale Opportunities

Exclusive presale opportunities, such as those offered to Mastercard cardholders, can impact ticket availability and pricing.

These presale windows provide a unique chance for cardholders to secure tickets before they are made available to the general public, potentially influencing ticket availability for specific matches and sessions.

Premium Experiences and Special Events

The availability and pricing of premium experiences, such as gala evenings and exclusive packages, may be influenced by the limited capacity and the added benefits and amenities included in these packages.

Special events like Yannick Noah Day also offer unique opportunities at a set price, with profits being allocated to charitable causes.

On the whole

Attending the French Open in 2024 offers a range of ticketing options, from the Opening Week and main draw to premium experiences and exclusive presale opportunities.

The ticket prices and availability are influenced by factors such as demand, timing of purchase, and the nature of the experiences offered.

Prospective attendees are encouraged to secure their tickets through authorized partners and to consider their preferences, budget, and desired experiences when making their ticketing decisions.

By providing a comprehensive overview of the ticket categories, pricing tiers, availability, and factors affecting ticket prices, this guide aims to equip tennis fans with the information needed to make informed decisions and fully enjoy the unparalleled experience of the French Open, where the world's best tennis talents converge to compete on the vibrant clay courts of Roland-Garros.

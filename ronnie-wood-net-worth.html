Tours Earnings

The Rolling Stones has headlined several highest-grossing tours over the years. Nonetheless, their A Bigger Bang Tour, which ran from 2005-to 2007, is considered one of the most successful tours of all time.

The band played 147 shows for 4,680,000 attendees with a $3.8 million average gross per show.

This totaled to $558,255,524 in box office collection which is considered the fourth-highest tour collection of all time.

Similarly, the Rolling Stones headlined the No Filter Tour from 2015 to 2021, in which they played 45 shows for 2,290,871 total attendees.

The tour made an average of $9.24 million per show and grossed a total of $415,600,000 at the box office.

Moreover, the band had the highest-grossing tour of the year in 2021, earning over $115 million in total, selling over 516,000 tickets for 12 shows across 11 cities.

Though we do not have the precise data on what the cut of each band member was from these tours, we can safely assume that they made a fortune given the success of the tours.

Ronnie Wood | Lifestyle

Ronnie enjoys a vibrant lifestyle with a boatload of money and a legacy that will last forever.

Well, nothing lasts forever!

Woody leads a comfortable lifestyle along with his family.

He has six children.

Wood was married to a former model Krissy from 1971 to 1978 and had a son with her.

It was during this time he began an affair with Pattie Boyd, George Harrison’s ex-wife.

Wood married his second wife, Jo Wood, in 1985. He had two children with Jo and also adopted her son Jamie from a previous relationship.

Jo filed for a divorce which was finalized in 2009.

Wood married his third and current wife, Sally Humphreys, on 20 December 2012. Sally is 31 years younger than Wood. Together they share twin girls named Alice Rose and Gracie Jane.

Similarly, Wood is a granddad to six grandchildren.

The legendary guitarist has also been frank about his struggle with alcohol addiction.

For his alcohol problem, he has been admitted to a rehabilitation center a few times.

As a matter of fact, ITN reported that Wood had checked himself into a rehabilitation center a total of six-time by 2008.

Furthermore, in 2017 Ronnie was diagnosed with lung cancer, which necessitated the partial removal of his lung.

He refused chemotherapy because of his hair which he did not want to lose.

In April of 2021, it was announced that Ronnie had been diagnosed with small-cell cancer.

Nevertheless, he has been deemed all-clear by doctors.

As of 2022, Ronnie has been sober for 11 years.

Moreover, Ronnie is into sports and a huge fan of the EFL Championship soccer club West Bromwich Albion.

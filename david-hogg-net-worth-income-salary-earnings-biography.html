David Hogg Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

David Hogg (August 21, 1886 – October 23, 1973) was an American politician who represented the state of Indiana in the United States Congress. Hogg was raised in the small town of Crothersville, Indiana, and attended the local public schools. He received his bachelor’s degree from Indiana University College of Liberal Arts in Bloomington in 1909, and his law degree from Indiana University School of Law in 1912.  He was called to the Indiana bar in 1913 and began practising law in Fort Wayne, Indiana, the following year. During his tenure as chairman of the Allen County Republican Committee, he was elected to a second term. In the Sixty-ninth Congress (March 4, 1925 – March 3, 1933), Hogg was re-elected as a Republican, and he served in the following three Congresses as well. He ran unsuccessfully for reelection to the Seventy-third Congress in 1932, for reelection to the Seventy-fourth Congress in 1934, and for reelection to the Seventy-fifth Congress in 1936, all of which were unsuccessful. He returned to his former profession as an attorney. This business was established as a mutual life insurance company in 1939. From 1940 until 1943, he served as president of the Goodwill Industries of Fort Wayne. From 1941 until 1946, he worked as a co-publisher of an interdenominational newspaper. He returned to the profession of law after a brief hiatus. He lived in Fort Wayne, Indiana, until his death on October 23, 1973, at the age of 63. Lindenwood Cemetery is where he was laid to rest.

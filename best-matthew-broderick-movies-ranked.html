List of the Best Matthew Broderick Movies Ranked Matthew Broderick Filmography

Matthew Broderick is a versatile actor whose talent has spanned various film genres. From comedies to dramas, his performances have left an indelible mark on the silver screen. But which movies truly stand out as his best? In this article, we will rank the top Matthew Broderick movies, taking a closer look at his filmography and celebrating his incredible talent.

Key Takeaways:

  • Matthew Broderick’s filmography showcases his versatility as an actor.
  • His performance as Ferris Bueller in “Ferris Bueller’s Day Off” remains one of his most iconic roles.
  • “WarGames” demonstrates Broderick’s ability to captivate audiences in thrilling and suspenseful films.
  • Broderick’s portrayal of Leo Bloom in the film adaptation of “The Producers” is both hilarious and endearing.
  • “Glory” highlights Broderick’s skill in portraying complex characters and delving into emotional depth.

What are the other movies that deserve a spot on the list? Dive into the article to discover the best Matthew Broderick movies ranked and explore his impressive filmography.

Ferris Bueller’s Day Off (1986)

Ferris Bueller’s Day Off is one of Matthew Broderick’s most iconic films. In this classic comedy, Broderick plays the charismatic high school student Ferris Bueller, who decides to skip school and embark on a series of adventurous escapades in Chicago. Broderick’s portrayal of Ferris showcases his charm and wit, making it one of his best performances.

WarGames (1983)

In the captivating film WarGames, Matthew Broderick takes on the role of David Lightman, a tech-savvy teenager whose innocent curiosity leads him to discover a military supercomputer.

Unaware of the potentially catastrophic consequences, David unwittingly sets off a chain of events that could result in nuclear war. Broderick’s performance as David Lightman showcases his ability to captivate audiences with his portrayal of a young protagonist caught in a dangerous situation. His charisma and vulnerability make him a relatable and compelling character.

Broderick’s portrayal of David Lightman in WarGames is a testament to his talent as an actor. He immerses himself in the role, capturing the fear, excitement, and determination of a young man confronting the unintended consequences of his actions.

This thrilling film explores the high-stakes world of computer hacking and raises important questions about the potential dangers of technology. Broderick’s performance adds depth and authenticity to the story, making WarGames one of the standout films in his illustrious career.

As one of his early breakthrough roles, WarGames solidified Broderick’s position as a rising star in Hollywood. His portrayal of David Lightman remains memorable and serves as a testament to his talent and range as an actor.

    • The Producers (2005)

    In the film adaptation of the hit Broadway musical, Matthew Broderick takes on the role of Leo Bloom once again, reprising his Tony-nominated performance. Broderick’s portrayal of Leo, a timid accountant turned Broadway producer, is a delightful blend of humor and charm. His comedic timing and endearing performance elevate the film to new heights.

    The Producers is a musical comedy that centers around the outrageous antics of Leo and his eccentric partner Max Bialystock, played by Nathan Lane. The chemistry between Broderick and Lane is palpable, creating a dynamic duo that brings the characters to life on the big screen.

    “I want everything I’ve ever seen in the movies!” – Leo Bloom

    Broderick’s ability to embody Leo’s transformation from a timid accountant to a confident Broadway producer is a testament to his talent as an actor. His portrayal captures the vulnerability, wit, and determination of the character, earning him critical acclaim and solidifying his place in the rankings of Matthew Broderick’s best movies.

    Glory (1989)

    Matthew Broderick delivers a captivating performance in the historical drama, Glory. Set during the American Civil War, the film follows Captain Robert Shaw as he leads an all-Black Union Army regiment, showcasing Broderick’s ability to portray complex characters with depth and emotional range. His portrayal of Shaw in Glory is a testament to his talent and solidifies the film’s place in Broderick’s impressive filmography.

    This critically acclaimed film captures the courage and sacrifice of the men fighting for their freedom, while highlighting the challenges and prejudices they faced. Broderick’s performance as Shaw showcases his dedication to bringing authenticity and humanity to his roles, making Glory a powerful and thought-provoking film that continues to resonate with audiences.

    “Matthew Broderick’s portrayal of Captain Robert Shaw in Glory is a true testament to his talent as an actor. He brings a captivating mix of determination, vulnerability, and leadership to the role, captivating audiences with his exceptional performance.” – Movie Critic

    With its powerful storytelling, compelling performances, and impactful themes, Glory stands as one of the standout films in Matthew Broderick’s career. His portrayal of Captain Robert Shaw in this historical epic cements his status as a versatile and talented actor.

    Biloxi Blues (1988)

    Biloxi Blues, adapted from Neil Simon’s play, showcases Matthew Broderick’s best performances as he portrays Eugene Morris Jerome, a young man drafted into the Army during World War II. Broderick’s portrayal of Eugene is a masterclass in capturing the complexities of a character’s journey.

    Throughout the film, Broderick brings depth, vulnerability, and wit to the role, immersing audiences in Eugene’s experiences of self-discovery, maturity, and the challenges faced by soldiers during wartime. The emotional range and nuanced performance by Broderick make Biloxi Blues a standout in his filmography.

    Broderick’s ability to seamlessly transition from humorous moments to poignant reflections demonstrates his versatility as an actor. His portrayal of Eugene not only captures the character’s individual growth but also reflects the universal struggles of young men thrust into the realities of war.

    “Biloxi Blues showcases Matthew Broderick’s ability to deliver a captivating performance that effortlessly balances humor and drama. His portrayal of Eugene Morris Jerome is a testament to his versatility and talent as an actor.”

    Broderick’s distinctive style and impeccable comedic timing shine through in Biloxi Blues, providing both entertainment and thought-provoking moments for the audience. His chemistry with the ensemble cast enhances the overall quality of the film, creating a truly memorable cinematic experience.

    The Lion King (1994)

Broderick’s vocal performance brings depth and emotion to the young lion prince as he navigates his journey to become the rightful king. His portrayal of Simba adds to the film’s timeless appeal and showcases Broderick’s talent in animated films.

    Simba’s journey in The Lion King captures the hearts of audiences of all ages. From his carefree days as a cub to his ultimate destiny as king, Broderick’s nuanced performance captures the range of emotions that Simba experiences. Whether it’s delivering heartfelt lines or singing memorable songs such as “Hakuna Matata” and “Can You Feel the Love Tonight,” Broderick’s voice brings Simba to life in a way that is both captivating and moving.

    You Can Count on Me (2000)

    In the film You Can Count on Me, Matthew Broderick delivers a standout performance as Brian, a supportive boss and love interest to the film’s protagonist, Sammy Prescott. Broderick’s portrayal of Brian is nuanced and compelling, showcasing his ability to bring depth to supporting roles.

    You Can Count on Me is a character study that allows Broderick to showcase his dramatic range and acting prowess. The film delves into the complexities of human relationships and explores themes of loyalty, family, and personal growth.

    “Matthew Broderick’s performance in You Can Count on Me is a masterclass in subtle acting. He effortlessly captures the conflicts and vulnerabilities of his character, making Brian a relatable and memorable presence in the film.” – Film Critic

    Broderick’s performance in You Can Count on Me highlights his career as a versatile actor, able to excel in both leading and supporting roles. His ability to bring authenticity and emotional depth to his characters has solidified his reputation as one of the most talented actors in the industry.

    The Cable Guy (1996)

    In the dark comedy film The Cable Guy, Matthew Broderick takes on the role of Steven, a regular guy who becomes unwittingly entangled in a bizarre and twisted relationship with an eccentric cable TV installer named Chip, played by Jim Carrey. As the story unfolds, Steven finds himself caught in a web of manipulation and pranks orchestrated by Chip, who becomes increasingly obsessed with their newfound friendship.

    Broderick’s performance as Steven in The Cable Guy is a masterclass in playing offbeat characters with a natural ease. He flawlessly balances the tension and comedy of the film, effortlessly switching between moments of humor and moments of genuine unease. Broderick’s portrayal adds depth to the character of Steven, allowing the audience to empathize with his plight while also finding humor in the absurdity of the situation.

    Directed by Ben Stiller and written by Judd Apatow, The Cable Guy is a unique dark comedy that pushes the boundaries of traditional film genres. It showcases Broderick’s versatility as an actor, allowing him to break away from his usual on-screen persona and delve into a more complex and unconventional role.

    “The Cable Guy is a one-of-a-kind film that combines dark humor, suspense, and social commentary. Matthew Broderick delivers a standout performance as Steven, capturing the character’s vulnerability and confusion with precision.”

    Released in 1996, The Cable Guy was met with mixed reviews upon its initial release but has since gained a cult following. The film’s unique blend of comedy and darkness, coupled with the dynamic performances of Broderick and Carrey, has allowed it to endure as a cult classic in the realm of offbeat comedies.

    Whether you’re a fan of Matthew Broderick’s work or simply enjoy dark comedies with a twist, The Cable Guy is a must-watch. Broderick’s performance as Steven is a standout in his filmography, showcasing his ability to take on unconventional roles and deliver captivating performances.

    Manchester by the Sea (2016)

    Matthew Broderick’s performance in Manchester by the Sea may be small in terms of screen time, but it is undoubtedly significant. In this critically acclaimed film, Broderick portrays Jeffrey, the fiancé of one of the main characters. Despite the limited role, Broderick manages to leave a lasting impression with his portrayal, adding depth and complexity to his character.

    This film showcases Broderick’s ability to make an impact even in a supporting role, highlighting his talent as a versatile actor. Manchester by the Sea is a powerful and emotionally charged drama that captivates audiences with its raw portrayal of grief and redemption. Broderick’s performance shines amidst the ensemble cast, further solidifying his place in the ranks of talented actors.

    Matthew Broderick’s role in Manchester by the Sea serves as a testament to his acting prowess and his ability to shine in any role he takes on. Despite the limited screen time, his performance resonates with authenticity and leaves a lasting impression on viewers.

    Painkiller (2023)

    Painkiller is a highly anticipated miniseries that premiered on Netflix, featuring the incredible talent of Matthew Broderick. In this gripping drama, Broderick takes on the role of Richard Sackler, the former co-chairman and president of Purdue Pharma.

    Broderick’s portrayal of Sackler is truly remarkable, as he delves into the complexities of this morally ambiguous character. His performance is both chilling and thought-provoking, showcasing his ability to bring depth and authenticity to every role he takes on.

    Painkiller adds another impressive entry to Matthew Broderick’s already stellar filmography. Known for his versatility as an actor, Broderick continues to captivate audiences with his ability to tackle complex and challenging roles, leaving a lasting impact with his performances.

Introduction 

Holly Holm is an American mixed martial artist who previously held the UFC Women’s Bantamweight Championship.

She was a professional boxer and kickboxer before entering the MMA, making her UFC debut in February 2015.

As of January 2024, Holly Holm’s net worth is estimated to be $4 Million.

Holly Holm Facts

  • Holm was born in Albuquerque, New Mexico, and grew up in the city.
  • She started as an amateur kickboxer in her teens, amassing a 6-0-2 record.
  • In 2002, Holm made her professional kickboxing debut with a technical knockout win over Valerie Anthonson.
  • In the same year, Holm won her first professional boxing match against Martha Deitchman.
  • Holm finally entered mixed martial arts in 2011 and won her debut in her home city.

Early Life 

Holly Rene Holm was born October 17, 1981 in Albuquerque, New Mexico and lived in the city throughout her entire childhood.

Holm’s is the daughter of Tammy and Roger Holm, with her father being a preacher in Albuquerque and her mother working as a massage therapist.

Active in several sports, Holm excelled in swimming, gymnastics, and soccer, but found her true calling in combat sports while attending Manzano High School.

Following her high school graduation, Holm attended the University of New Mexico, but her amateur fighting career was starting to take off so she dropped out to focus on her training.

Career 

In January 2002, Holm made her professional boxing debut with a technical knockout, winning her first five career bouts.

Holm would have a couple of draws in boxing, but would only lose two matches as a pro, against Rita Turrisi (2004) and Ann Sophie Mathis (2011).

In the same year, she suffered her final boxing loss, Holm began her professional MMA career, defeating Christina Domke via technical knockout.

Holm would go on to win her first nine matches, including her two initial Ultimate Fighting Championship matches against Raquel Pennington and Marion Reneau, earning the praises of Gina Carano and other female legends.

In November 2015, Holm pulled off a massive upset by defeating Ronda Rousey via knockout in the second round to win the UFC Women’s Bantamweight Championship.

Holm would, unfortunately, go on a cold stretch while training with Joanna Jedrzejczyk, losing six of her next 10 matches, including dropping her title to Miesha Tate less than four months after winning.

After her upset over Rousey, Holm would score wins over Bethe Correia and Megan Anderson while dropping matches to Amanda Nunes and Cris Cyborg.

Holly Holm’s Career Earnings & Net Worth

During the start of her professional MMA career, Holm was making enough money to get by, but it wasn’t until she made it to UFC that she started earning big money.

In her UFC debut against Raquel Pennington, Holm earned $50,000, including $25,000 for the win bonus, and earned another $50,000 for the follow-up match against Marion Reneau.

Of course, Holm’s big payout came from when she defeated Ronda Rousey as she was paid $165,000 in total, which included a $50,000 bonus for the best fight of the night and $40,000 from Reebok.

As champion, Holm earned a half million from her fight against Miesha Tate, and she would continue to earn hundreds of thousands for her subsequent UFC bouts.

Due to a fairly frugal lifestyle, her many professional matches, and endorsements Holly Holm has had, she is worth an estimated $4 million.

Personal Life

Holly Holm was raised by her parents Roger and Tammy alongside her brother Weston Holm, all of whom she maintains a close relationship with.

She does not have children, and is not currently married, but she was wedded to college sweetheart Jeff Kirkpatrick from 2012 until 2020 when they filed for divorce and she obtained her maiden name.

The divorce was said to be a rather amicable one, with neither party asking for alimony payments and not having children avoided any child support disagreements.

Holm has an official website and is active on social media with an official Twitter account and official Instagram account that are both updated regularly. 

Awards & Achievements

As she’s proven that she can win in several different forms of combat sports, Holly Holm is one of the most decorated fighters of her time.

Ever since her amateur career, Holm has been racking up wins and winning belts, which continued into her time with the UFC.

Here are the most prestigious awards and achievements from Holly Holm’s career:

  • First-ever Legacy FC Women’s Bantamweight Division Champion.
  • UFC Women’s Bantamweight Championship holder from November 15, 2015 to March 5, 2016.
  • Two-time Performance of the Night winner (against Ronda Rousey and Bethe Correia).

Career

  • McCants was the first player chosen in the 1990 NFL draft. Still, the Atlanta Falcons backed down owing to reports of knee problems and accusations that a sports agent had paid his family money while still in college.

  • McCants received a 5-year, $7.4 million contract with the Buccaneers after being chosen fourth overall in 1990, including a $2.5 million cash signing bonus.
  • The Buccaneers hired Floyd Peters in 1991 and transformed McCants from a linebacker to a defensive end.
  • Although McCants opposed the shift, Peters persuaded him that with time, he might become a success story similar to Chris Doleman, another Peters convert.
  • In 1993 while with the Houston Oilers, McCants stepped in to separate Buddy Ryan and Kevin Gilbride when Ryan punched Gilbride in the face following an Oilers fumble.
